# Overview

Battery Self-Discharge Current (SDC) is the small amount of electrical current that is lost naturally from a battery when it is not in use, due to internal chemical reactions within the battery. Measuring SDC accurately helps in understanding the health and efficiency of a battery, allowing manufacturers and users to predict battery life and performance more effectively.

# Principle

The principle of SDC measurement involves understanding the internal chemical processes of a battery that lead to current loss when the battery is not in use.

# Key Parameters

| Parameter | Description |
|-----------|-------------|
| Self-Discharge Current | The current lost from a battery when not in use due to internal reactions. |

# Applications

| Application | Description |
|-------------|-------------|
| Battery Storage | Monitoring SDC during storage to prevent thermal runaway. |
| Battery Manufacturing | Identifying defective batteries with higher SDC in production batches. |
| Battery Operation | Detecting abnormal behavior in battery systems to prevent failures. |

# Related Techniques

| Technique | Description |
|-----------|-------------|
| Traditional Method | Estimating SDC by monitoring open circuit voltage drop over time. |
| Potentiostatic Method | Using a voltage source to measure SDC by applying the battery's OCV. |
| Galvanostatic Method | Applying small DC currents to measure SDC and dynamic capacitance. |
| Galvanostatic Parallel Method | Measuring SDC of multiple batteries in parallel for faster electrochemical balance. |

# Notes

* Arbin SDC monitoring feature is designed for battery storage, battery manufacturing, and battery operation. The procedure of a battery getting bad usually takes a long time. Continuous monitoring of SDC of cells along with the life of the battery provides early detection of problematic cells and prevents hazardous thermal runaway from happening.
* The SDC monitoring does not require active power sources, making it suitable for battery storage and shipping.
* The self-discharge current of batteries from the same production batch should be similar, allowing for easy identification of defective batteries.
* The SDC monitoring can greatly reduce the risk of thermal runaway and resulting fire, potentially lowering safety measure costs in battery manufacturing.
