# Overview

The US renewable energy industry has been growing steadily in the past two decades. From 2000 to 2018, renewable energy grew 100% and in 2020, renewable sources made up 11.4% of the country’s energy source. With the government’s new initiative for the power sector to be decarbonized by 2035, the shift towards clean energy will most definitely accelerate.

# Detailed Explanation

Electrification across the board is essential for decarbonization. Transportation is a major contributor to carbon pollution, and the shift towards electric vehicles (EVs) is gaining momentum. Many cities are replacing public buses with electric alternatives, and companies like FedEx and UPS are acquiring electric vehicles to electrify their fleets.

Buildings and homes are also becoming more electrified, with gas-powered appliances being replaced by electric ones. Studies indicate that gas-powered appliances increase indoor air pollution, prompting a shift towards induction stoves and other electric alternatives. Although the initial cost of replacing technology with electric versions can be high, studies have shown that they are more cost-efficient in the long run.

Supportive infrastructure is crucial for electrification. A modernized grid and energy storage are necessary to ensure a consistent supply of electricity, especially given the erratic availability of renewable sources like solar and wind. It is estimated that at least 3.6 gigawatts of battery storage will be installed in 2021, enhancing grid resilience and the ability to integrate multiple energy sources.

Expanding electric vehicle charging facilities is vital for creating an EV-friendly environment, which encourages carbon-emission free options such as light-rails or bicycle paths. These options contribute to decarbonization and promote clean commuting.

One of the advantages of renewable energy is that individuals can contribute to its generation. Solar PV is an accessible way for homes and businesses to adopt renewable energy. The installation of residential energy storage is expected to grow six-fold by 2025, indicating a willingness among individuals to take clean energy into their own hands. Updating to a modern two-way grid will allow homes and buildings to contribute to energy production and sell electricity back to the grid.
