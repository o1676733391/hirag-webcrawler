# Detailed Explanation

Electric trucks are becoming a significant part of the electric vehicle (EV) movement, challenging the traditional views of truck performance. Many misconceptions exist, such as the belief that electric trucks are less powerful or have inferior handling. However, advancements in electric motor technology have led to trucks that can deliver impressive torque and towing capabilities, making them suitable for heavy-duty applications.

Currently, several legacy car manufacturers, including Ford and Chevy, are planning to convert their traditional pickup lines into electric offerings. Additionally, companies like Tesla and Rivian are leading the charge with innovative electric truck designs.

## The Tesla Cybertruck

Tesla's truck offering features:
- A durable exoskeleton made from cold-rolled stainless steel and Tesla armor glass
- Up to 3,500 pounds of payload capacity
- 100 cubic feet of exterior, lockable storage
- A towing capacity of over 14,000 pounds
- Adaptive air suspension
- 0-60 mph in as little as 2.9 seconds
- 500 miles of range

## The Rivian R1T

The Rivian R1T offers:
- 400+ miles of range
- 0-60 mph in three seconds
- A quad motor system
- A wading depth of over three feet
- A towing capacity of 11,000 pounds
- Up to 750 horsepower
- Extensive lockable storage
