# Overview

Chinese car battery manufacturers CALT announced that it is ready to produce a battery that can last for 1.2 million miles across the span of 16 years. This is double the lifespan and eight-times the mileage of current EV batteries, the best of which are typically warrantied for up to 150,000 miles for 8-10 years. This development implies that CATL has figured out a way to reduce battery degradation and loss of capacity — a process that occurs naturally with batteries over time.

A battery of this caliber could change the EV industry for the better. It has been long thought that battery technology has yet to reach the capabilities necessary to overtake internal combustion engine (ICE) vehicles for good. While the first generation of fully electric vehicles are still coming to age, there is not yet a comprehensive idea of the impact of long-term consumer use on EVs and batteries. Nonetheless, a battery of this capacity could significantly drive forward EV adoption and provoke larger energy storage solutions.

# Detailed Explanation

The million-mile battery could significantly drive forward EV adoption and provoke larger energy storage solutions. It is believed that this battery could help bring down battery costs, which currently range from $175 to $300 per kilowatt-hour. Analysts suggest that for EV prices to be comparable to ICE vehicles, battery costs would need to decline to $100 per kilowatt-hour. However, a report from the MIT Energy Initiative indicates that this price point may not be reached soon if manufacturers continue to rely on lithium-ion batteries.

CATL’s new battery is priced at a 10% premium compared to their current EV batteries. Despite the higher upfront cost, the longevity of the battery could alleviate concerns regarding the total cost of ownership, as EV batteries account for a significant portion of the vehicle's cost. The million-mile battery may not address range anxiety, as the capacity per charge is still dependent on the battery size.

The million-mile battery could also lead to advancements in battery recycling and repurposing, as it may outlast the vehicles it powers. This could further decrease energy storage costs. Additionally, heavy-duty applications such as taxis, buses, and trucks would benefit from the durability of this battery, which can endure more stress than private passenger vehicles.

The future of EV batteries looks promising, with manufacturers exploring solid-state electrolytes, heavy-metal free batteries, ultra-fast charging, and higher energy capacity solutions to address the global push towards clean energy.
