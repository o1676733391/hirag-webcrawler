# Overview

Market analysts predict that 705,000 tons of lithium-ion batteries will reach end-of-life by 2025. Despite the increasing battery waste, the battery recycling rate is considerably low — it is currently estimated to only be 5%.

# Notes

* Cheaper raw materials can make recycling economically unviable.
* Complex chemistries complicate the recycling process.
* Difficult processes due to battery structure hinder efficient recycling.
* Proper battery waste handling can prevent environmental pollution.
* Increasing supply of recycled metals can reduce the need for mining.
* Governments and private companies are working to improve battery recycling.
