# Overview

A battery management system allows users to monitor individual cells within a battery pack, maintaining stability throughout the whole pack and protecting it from damage, prolonging its life, and keeping the battery operating within its safety limits.

# Key Features

| Feature | Description |
|---------|-------------|
| Monitoring | Constant monitoring of individual cells within a battery pack. |
| Protection | Protects the battery from damage and prolongs its life. |
| Communication | Communicates information to an external interface for user observation. |
| Measurement | Measures current, voltage, temperature, and coulomb count. |
| State of Charge (SoC) | Indicates the remaining energy in the battery. |
| State of Health (SoH) | Assesses the long-term capabilities of the battery. |
| Cell Balancing | Equalizes charge between individual cells to maintain stability. |

# Notes

* Coulomb counting helps estimate the available capacity of a battery.
* State of Charge (SoC) and State of Health (SoH) are important indicators for assessing battery usability.
* The BMS must keep a record of initial conditions and measurements throughout the battery's lifecycle.
* Lithium cells require careful monitoring to ensure safe operation.
* Cell balancing is crucial for maintaining the health of the overall battery pack.
* Good battery testing software is essential for effective monitoring and analysis.
