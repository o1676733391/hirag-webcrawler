# Notes

* Material costs greatly affect the feasibility of using a certain material within batteries.
* In 2010, a lithium-ion battery pack cost more than USD 1000/kWh. This dropped significantly to USD 156/kWh by 2019, and is projected to drop below USD 100/kWh in the coming years.
* Certain materials commonly used within lithium-ion batteries contribute to the high cost of the battery.
* Analysts predict that the supply of the rare metals used in current batteries could deteriorate in just a few years.
* The sodium-ion battery is potentially cheaper and easier to produce than its lithium-ion counterpart.
* Silicon tends to deteriorate quickly when used in a battery.
* The dendrites formed inside the battery could potentially pierce the separator and cause the battery to short-circuit.
* Certain materials are toxic to the environment, and improper disposal can lead to safety hazards.
* Lithium mining has been found to cause habitat destruction and pollution.
* Sustainability should be considered alongside cost and battery chemistries to find balanced and viable alternatives for batteries.
