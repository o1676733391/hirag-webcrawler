# Overview

Temperature has a significant impact on battery performance. A battery releases energy through electro-chemical reactions, which are encouraged by higher ambient temperatures. Different battery chemistries have different ranges of optimal operating temperatures.

# Notes

Continuous exposure to high temperatures can cause unwanted chemical reactions and result in gas, corrosion, or even fires and explosions. Moreover, a battery cell can enter into thermal runaway. If one cell in the pack heats up, this can quickly cause the whole pack to overheat, undermining the safety of the system. 

Battery testers and researchers need to establish how a battery would react under different temperatures to see if they are suitable for their intended application. Comprehensive testing under different temperatures helps determine which conditions can negatively impact battery performance.
