# Overview

The US renewable energy industry has been growing steadily in the past two decades. From 2000 to 2018, renewable energy grew 100% and in 2020, renewable sources made up 11.4% of the country’s energy source. With the government’s new initiative for the power-sector to be decarbonized by 2035, the shift towards clean energy will most definitely accelerate.

# Detailed Explanation

Electrification across the board is essential for decarbonization. Transportation is a major contributor to carbon pollution, and the shift towards electric vehicles (EVs) is gaining momentum. Cities are replacing public buses with electric alternatives, and delivery services like FedEx and UPS are transitioning to electric fleets.

Buildings and homes are also becoming more electrified, with gas-powered appliances being replaced by electric versions. Studies indicate that gas-powered appliances contribute to indoor air pollution, prompting a shift towards induction stoves and other electric alternatives. Although the initial cost of replacing technology can be high, studies have shown that electric options are more cost-efficient in the long run.

Supportive infrastructure is necessary to facilitate electrification. A modernized grid and energy storage solutions are critical to ensure a consistent supply of electricity, especially given the variability of renewable sources like solar and wind. It is projected that 3.6 gigawatts of battery storage will be installed in 2021, enhancing grid resilience.

Expanding electric vehicle charging facilities is vital for creating an EV-friendly environment, which encourages carbon-emission free options such as light-rails and bicycle paths. These initiatives contribute to decarbonization and promote clean commuting options.

Localization of energy generation allows individuals to contribute to renewable energy production. Solar PV adoption has surged, and the installation of residential energy storage is expected to grow significantly by 2025. A modern two-way grid will enable homes to produce energy and sell it back to the grid, fostering a resilient local energy system.
