# Overview

Arbin’s main products are advanced battery testing systems that accurately simulate real-world charge and discharge cycles. These systems help researchers, engineers, and manufacturers evaluate battery performance, safety, and lifespan under a wide range of conditions.

# Key Points

| Topic | Summary |
|-------|---------|
| Key Specifications | Two of the most important specifications of a testing system are its maximum channel voltage and maximum channel current. Together, these determine the limits at which the system can safely charge and discharge a battery – making them essential factors when selecting the right solution for your application. |
| LBTS Series | LBTS stands for Laboratory Battery Tester, and is actually the next generation of the original LBT series. This product line is divided into two categories: LBTS-Cell and LBTS-Module. |
| RBT4 Series | RBT4-Cell has a max current range of 100A – 400A per channel (parallel up to 1600A) and a max voltage of 20V. RBT4-Module has a current range of 75A – 300A per channel (parallel up to 1200A) and a max voltage range of 30V – 200V. |
| RBT3 | RBT3 offers the highest current and voltage ranges of all Arbin products with a max current range of 150A – 400A per channel (parallel to achieve thousands of amps) and a max voltage range of 300V – 1000V. |
| Parallel Capability | One of Arbin’s advantages is the ability to parallel channels to achieve higher current. For example, you can combine four 400A channels to achieve 1600A. Higher voltage may also be achieved by hardware serializing multiple channels (RBT series only). |

# Detailed Explanation

This article provides an overview of the voltage ranges and current ranges available across Arbin’s main product lines. In a follow-up article, we will explore Arbin’s auxiliary products, which further expand testing capabilities.

Arbin Product Overview Graph above illustrates the current and voltage ranges of Arbin’s main products. However, a bit of elaboration will help clarify the details:

You can see that RBT4 maximum voltage is only up to 200V. Therefore, when you want to test something higher than 200V but still less than 300V, we will need to introduce RBT3 300V, this will also cover the voltage range that is bigger than 200V but still smaller than 300V.
