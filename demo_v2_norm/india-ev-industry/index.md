# Overview

India is one of the leading Asian countries in electric vehicle adoption. Currently, the country aims to have 30% of its road vehicles be electric by 2030. Not only is this a step toward a greener future, it is also to address one of the country’s top issues — pollution. India is one of the top polluting countries in the world, and by reducing the amount of tailpipe emissions on the roads, it hopes to rectify this problem.

# Detailed Explanation

As cities grow, the number of vehicles on the road increases, leading to more roadside pollution. This has become a pressing health concern in India. The government is pushing for all new three-wheeled vehicles to be electric by 2023, and by 2025 for two-wheelers. This has offered startups and companies the opportunity to build ecosystems that support and regulate the change.

## Creative Opportunities

However massive these roadblocks seem, they offer opportunities to develop creative technological solutions to address India’s specific circumstances. Various companies are taking initiatives to tackle the growing pains of the electrification of mobility in the country.

### Two and three-wheeled EVs

More than 80% of road vehicles and 95% of EV sales in India are two-wheelers. The government is pushing for all new three-wheeled vehicles to be electric by 2023, and by 2025 for two-wheelers.

### Locally Developed and Manufactured Batteries

One of the common issues with EVs in India are the batteries. With a much hotter climate than western nations, batteries overheat much more easily. The government is pushing “Make in India” initiatives to encourage local manufacturing, providing opportunities for research and development into batteries that are adapted to the needs and environment of the country.

### Shared Mobility Solutions

Studies have shown that there is a shift towards shared mobility services in India, especially among the younger generation. The government is pushing companies like Uber and Ola to make 40% of their fleets electric by 2026.

### Battery Swapping

Battery swapping is seen as a less disruptive solution for charging EVs, addressing the issue of the lack of space for charging facilities. For instance, Ola has 14 battery swapping stations where e-rickshaw drivers can swap batteries.
